The Accordion

The accordion is a graphical control element comprising a vertically stacked list of items, such as labels or thumbnails. Each item can be "expanded" or "collapsed" to reveal the content associated with that item.

Accordions organize related information into smaller sections, reduce visual clutter, and save screen space, especially on mobile devices or side panels. Avoid using accordions when users need to see all content, when content is simple or non-hierarchical, or if it obscures important elements.

An accordion is an interactive user interface element that organizes content into stacked, collapsible sections. Users click on a heading or title to expand or collapse the content panel beneath it, allowing for a more compact and manageable display of information.

In most designs, clicking a collapsed card will trigger the currently open card to collapse, and then the clicked card will expand. This creates a smooth, focused user experience.

To choose an accordion expansion icon, select familiar icons like a plus sign (+) for expansion or a caret/chevron (down arrow for collapse, up arrow for expand) to indicate direction. The most crucial factor is a clear affordance that tells users the item is clickable and will expand.
